Complete the Square
1
The expression \(5{x}^{2}+7x-2\) fits the form \(a{x}^{2}+bx+c\). Let's complete the square, where:
\[\begin{aligned}&a=5\\&b=7\\&c=-2\end{aligned}\]

2
Factor out \(a\), which is \(5\).
\[5({x}^{2}+\frac{7}{5}x-\frac{2}{5})\]

3
Introduce the constant \(k\), which is \(\frac{49}{100}\) in our case.
1
Let \(k={(\frac{b}{2a})}^{2}\). From the earlier step, \(a=5\) and \(b=7\).
\[k={(\frac{b}{2a})}^{2}={(\frac{7}{2\times 5})}^{2}\]

2
Simplify.
\[k=\frac{49}{100}\]

\[5({(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}-\frac{49}{100}-\frac{2}{5})\]

5
Simplify.
\[5({(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}-\frac{89}{100})\]

6
Expand.
\[5{(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}-\frac{89}{20}\]

7
Substitute the above back into the original equation.
\[5{(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}-\frac{89}{20}=0\]

8
Add \(\frac{89}{20}\) to both sides.
\[5{(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}=\frac{89}{20}\]

9
Divide both sides by \(5\).
\[{(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}=\frac{\frac{89}{20}}{5}\]

10
Simplify  \(\frac{\frac{89}{20}}{5}\)  to  \(\frac{89}{20\times 5}\).
\[{(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}=\frac{89}{20\times 5}\]

11
Simplify  \(20\times 5\)  to  \(100\).
\[{(x+\frac{7}{10})}^{2}=\frac{89}{100}\]

12
Take the square root of both sides.
\[x+\frac{7}{10}=\pm \sqrt{\frac{89}{100}}\]

13
Simplify  \(\sqrt{\frac{89}{100}}\)  to  \(\frac{\sqrt{89}}{\sqrt{100}}\).
\[x+\frac{7}{10}=\pm \frac{\sqrt{89}}{\sqrt{100}}\]

14
Since \(10\times 10=100\), the square root of \(100\) is \(10\).
\[x+\frac{7}{10}=\pm \frac{\sqrt{89}}{10}\]

15
Break down the problem into these 2 equations.
\[x+\frac{7}{10}=\frac{\sqrt{89}}{10}\]
\[x+\frac{7}{10}=-\frac{\sqrt{89}}{10}\]

16

\[x=\frac{\sqrt{89}}{10}-\frac{7}{10}\]
